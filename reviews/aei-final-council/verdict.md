## Verdict

**Reject in current form / not submission-ready. A substantially redesigned new submission could become suitable for AEI.**

The repository demonstrates a substantial OOP digital prototype for a bounded, authored, three-shaft design study. It includes useful benchmark infrastructure, multiple search implementations, positive-candidate checking, automated tests, and extensive exploratory computation.

The current evidence does not demonstrate complete bounded negative proof, valid confirmatory backlash interaction, admission-grade domain-wide CAE accuracy, an isolated engineering-informatics advance, industrial effectiveness, or topology generality. The merged robustness scenarios directly invalidate two headline cells and their dependent inference. The CP-SAT tolerance mismatch contradicts completeness claims for positive tolerance. A historical clean attestation and byte/count robustness verification cannot cure either source-level defect.

The selected fully digital AEI scope is viable. Physical experiments are not a mandatory decision condition. The replacement evidence must instead include independent digital cases or published briefs, trusted computational/domain references, semantic evidence reconstruction, and externally reproducible execution.

## Decision Conditions

### Gate 0 - Withdraw Invalid Evidence

1. Mark robustness-confirmatory-v2 and affected manuscript claims superseded.
2. Identify every result using CP-SAT with positive ratio tolerance and withdraw its completeness/negative-proof status until regenerated.
3. Prevent publication/readiness verification from passing while a known-invalid bundle is referenced.

### Gate 1 - Correctness and Trust Boundaries

1. Implement injective scenario identity, uniqueness/cardinality invariants, and an independent streaming semantic robustness checker.
2. Encode ratio tolerance soundly in CP-SAT integer arithmetic or reject nonzero tolerance fail-closed.
3. Add an OOP composite validator for the complete `ProblemSpecification`, with mutation tests for every solver-visible field.
4. Use immutable certificates with explicit component model versions.
5. Emit a replayable bounded-search coverage/elimination ledger with a separate checker, or replace formal-proof terminology throughout.

### Gate 2 - Domain Model Validity

1. Correct mesh-level equal-and-opposite contact-force propagation and represent efficiency losses separately.
2. Establish a production mesh/error rule using representative boundary cases and prove admission classification stability; otherwise remove CAE admission claims and retain only screening language.
3. Rename the backlash variable to the modeled center-distance-induced increment or implement operating involute geometry and contact checks.
4. State and justify one strength limit-state equation; run threshold sensitivity.
5. Benchmark representative root-stress cases against a trusted independently implemented digital reference or applicable standard calculation.

### Gate 3 - Confirmatory Statistics

1. Declare target populations and estimands; select held-out layouts by seeded probability or justified stratification.
2. Preregister primary interaction/equivalence contrasts, practical margins, decision rules, and multiplicity handling.
3. Use multiple randomized QMC/Sobol scrambles, draw-count convergence, nested uncertainty, and precision-aware reporting.
4. Replace zero-width zero-event intervals with appropriate one-sided bounds.
5. Define the Sobol aggregation estimand and report layout dispersion and numerical uncertainty.

### Gate 4 - AEI Scientific Contribution

1. Formulate falsifiable hypotheses about executable engineering knowledge, traceability, and error detection.
2. Compare the graph/schema approach with a flat parameter/constraint representation and an established independent formal or engineering baseline.
3. Run representation/rule/certificate ablations and measure competency-question coverage, authoring and extension effort, defect localization, interoperability, and reuse.
4. Add at least one independently authored or published held-out digital case and one structurally different topology, with blind conversion and frozen acceptance criteria.
5. Integrate robustness into synthesis/admission and compare nominal with tolerance-aware synthesis on held-out cases.
6. Measure at least one practical workflow endpoint and present one end-to-end artifact-to-decision case.

### Gate 5 - Reproducibility and Release

1. Replace historical self-consistency verification with a fail-closed OOP attestation policy binding current tree, commit policy, explicit locks, commands, exits, logs, and inventories.
2. Provide one locked/containerized orchestration command that regenerates all scientific outputs into fresh directories and compares declared outputs/tolerances.
3. Adopt a shared immutable `ScientificArtifactManifest` across submission-critical evidence.
4. Execute the release workflow in public CI or an independent worker and retain inspectable normalized evidence.
5. Publish an installable versioned API/CLI with an external-consumer integration test.
6. Complete a reproducible closest-method literature protocol and an archived licensed release with DOI and citation metadata.

## Submission Threshold

Do not submit to AEI after only repairing L01/L02. Those fixes restore internal correctness but do not establish the journal contribution. A credible submission requires Gates 0-3 and 5, plus a completed AEI hypothesis/baseline study and independent digital validation from Gate 4. If structural-topology and independent-case evidence cannot be obtained, the manuscript must consistently present itself as a bounded methodological proof of concept and target a venue receptive to that narrower evidence level.
