# AEI Closest-Method Matrix and Claim Register

Search cutoff: `2026-07-11`

## Venue gate

[Advanced Engineering Informatics: Aims and Scope](https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/advanced-engineering-informatics): Explicit knowledge representation and use, engineering relevance, and vigorous evaluation of generality, power, and scalability.

## Search protocol

This is a structured closest-method audit, not a systematic review or a claim of exhaustive recall. Records and query families are frozen for reproducibility.

Query families:
- engineering design AND (knowledge graph OR executable knowledge OR formal semantics)
- engineering design synthesis AND (graph OR constraint programming)
- engineering design AND (infeasibility certificate OR infeasibility detection)
- gearbox OR gear train AND (automated design OR topology synthesis OR optimization)
- engineering informatics AND (reproducibility OR traceability OR algorithmic representation)

Inclusion rule: Include the closest method from each neighboring capability that overlaps knowledge representation, bounded synthesis, formal verification or infeasibility evidence, gear-domain automation, or traceable computational qualification.

Exclusion rule: Exclude methods lacking an artifact-centered engineering task or enough publisher metadata to determine synthesis and validation scope.

## Defensible contribution

The literature does not support novelty claims for graph-based design, gear optimization, knowledge formalization, constrained learning, or simulation-coupled generation by themselves. The candidate contribution is the integration of an executable engineering-knowledge schema, blind requirements-first bounded synthesis, independent positive certificates and complete negative proofs, and hash-bound reporting. The phrase `within the audited closest-method set` is mandatory because the matrix cannot prove a universal first.
